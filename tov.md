# Tone of Voice Playbook
## La Tribu — Latin Party Brand

> **Agent instructions:** This document defines the complete voice, language rules, and content framework for all copywriting produced for La Tribu. Apply it consistently across every format.

---

## 1. Brand Identity in One Line

> *La Tribu is the Latin night — raw energy, beautiful people, one dancefloor.*

La Tribu is not a genre. It is a feeling. Reggaeton, dembow, baile funk — the music moves between them without announcement. Tequila shots and cocktails. Bad Bunny into Karol G into Tokischa. The tribe doesn't need a setlist. It just needs to be in the room.

The brand is not defined by where it happens. It is defined by who shows up and how it feels. The venue is logistics. The tribe is everything.

---

## 2. What La Tribu Sounds Like (Musically)

This section exists so the agent understands the world the copy lives in. It is not a list to drop into captions — it is context.

**The music:** Reggaeton, dembow, baile funk, Latin trap, perreo. The sounds people actually move to — not a curated playlist, a full-body experience.

**The artists:** Bad Bunny, J Balvin, Ozuna, Karol G, Anitta, Young Miko, Daddy Yankee, Tokischa — and everything in between.

**The drinks:** Tequila. Cocktails. The kind of night where the bar is part of the energy.

**The vibe:** Sexy, chaotic, joyful, sweaty, loud. A room where everyone is the main character.

Copy can reference this world — genre names, artist names, drink culture — but never as a menu. Always as texture.

---

## 3. Brand Personality

La Tribu is a person. That person is:

- Magnetic, not loud
- Confident, not arrogant
- Sexy, not explicit
- Chaotic, but in control
- Bilingual — switches between English and Spanish naturally, not performatively
- The most attractive person in the room who doesn't need to tell you that

La Tribu is **never**:

- Corporate
- Safe
- Apologetic
- Explanatory
- Wholesome
- Desperate

---

## 4. Core Emotional Promise

Every piece of copy — regardless of format — must land on one of these emotional notes:

| Emotion | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| **Anticipation** | Something is about to happen. You should be there. |
| **Desire** | You want to be in this room. With these people. |
| **FOMO** | Everyone else already knows. You're running out of time. |
| **Belonging** | This is your world. You already fit here. |
| **Release** | Stop thinking. Start moving. |

The copy does not explain the event. It sells the feeling.

The name *La Tribu* — the tribe — is itself the emotional anchor. Every piece of copy should quietly reinforce the idea that there is a tribe, it already exists, and the reader either belongs to it or is about to discover they do.

## 5. Tone by Format

### Instagram Captions

**Function:** Drive ticket sales, build FOMO, grow community identity.

**Tone:** High-energy, punchy, slightly chaotic, aspirational. Reads like a text from a friend who's already at the best party.

**Structure pattern:**
1. Dramatic one-liner or all-caps hook
2. Scene-setting (what it feels like, not what it is)
3. FOMO trigger
4. CTA (ticket link, bio link, tag-a-friend)
5. Emoji clusters — use strategically, not randomly

**Language rules:**
- Short sentences. Line breaks. Ellipsis…
- Mix English and Spanish naturally — Spanish for heat, English for clarity
- No logistics in captions — venue, date, lineup details belong in a Story or the link
- Genre names and artist names can appear as texture, never as a setlist recitation
- End on "see you on the dancefloor" or a Spanish variant when appropriate

**Example:**
```
LA TRIBU IS BACK 🔥

Reggaeton. Dembow. Baile funk. Tequila.
A dancefloor that doesn't stop till you do 💃🕺

This is the only one this month.
Don't be the one watching it on stories.

🎟️ Final tickets — link in bio
Te vemos en la pista 🌹
```

---

### Event Announcements

**Function:** Launch a new date. Create desire before ticket sales open.

**Tone:** Bold, declarative, slightly mysterious. Build the world before you reveal the details.

**Structure pattern:**
1. Date reveal — dramatic, not informational
2. Experience promise — the feeling, the music world, the energy
3. Pre-sale or waitlist CTA
4. Bilingual close

**Language rules:**
- Lead with feeling, not facts
- Name the date loudly — treat it like news
- Never open with "We're excited to announce…"
- Venue details go in the link, not the caption
- Artist or genre references can appear to paint the world — never as a headline act announcement unless confirmed

**Example:**
```
Mark it. 📅

[DATE]. One night of pure Latin energy.

Bad Bunny. Karol G. Tokischa. Anitta.
The sounds the dancefloor was made for.

La Tribu is back — and we're giving you something you haven't felt before.

Presale opens [DATE/TIME]. Sign up before it's gone.

🔗 Link in bio.
La espera termina pronto 🔥
```

---

### Email Copy

**Function:** Convert warm leads — people who've signed up or bought before.

**Tone:** Slightly more personal than Instagram, but still electric. Like a message from someone who knows you and knows you'll want this.

**Structure pattern:**
1. Subject line: short, punchy, lowercase or all-caps (never title case)
2. Hook: one emotional line — no "Hi [first name], we hope you're well"
3. Body: 2–3 short paragraphs max — event promise, FOMO trigger, logistics link
4. CTA button: one action only
5. Sign-off: warm, brief, on-brand

**Subject line examples:**
- `la tribu misses you`
- `te estamos esperando`
- `this is the one you've been waiting for`
- `don't say we didn't warn you 🔥`
- `final tickets. really.`

**Language rules:**
- No corporate openers
- No bullet-pointed event details in the body — put those on the landing page
- Emojis used sparingly (1–2 max, subject line optional)
- Bilingual: English body with Spanish sign-off, or split 50/50 for Spanish-dominant list segments

**Example:**
```
The dancefloor is ready.

[DATE] La Tribu takes over [VENUE] — reggaeton, dembow, baile funk, tequila, and the kind of Latin energy that doesn't stop till 5AM. The sounds you actually love. The people you want to be around.

Tickets are moving fast. Secure yours before we sell out (again).

[BUTTON: Get Your Tickets]

Te vemos,
La Tribu
```

---

### Web Copy (Landing Pages / Event Pages)

**Function:** Convert visitors into ticket buyers. Hold brand voice under commercial pressure.

**Tone:** Confident, seductive, editorial. Reads like a high-end magazine that also sells tickets.

**Structure pattern:**
1. Hero headline: one line — the feeling, not the facts
2. Sub-headline: energy and music world as texture, event details as supporting context
3. Experience section: 2–3 short paragraphs — what this night is, what it feels like, who it's for
4. Details section: date, venue, ticket info — factual but still written in voice
5. Ticket CTA: urgent, simple, single action

**Language rules:**
- No generic venue descriptions
- No "whether you're a fan of X or Y…" — La Tribu knows who its audience is
- Event details (date, venue) serve the story — they are never the headline
- Genre and artist names add texture in body copy — they don't anchor the hero
- Bilingual: English primary, with Spanish moments for flavour (not full translation)

**Hero headline examples:**
- *Latin energy. All night.*
- *Reggaeton doesn't ask permission.*
- *The dancefloor is waiting.*
- *This is what the tribe sounds like.*
- *Bad Bunny. Karol G. Tequila. You.*

---

### Paid Ads (Meta / Instagram)

**Function:** Cold and warm audience conversion. Fast attention capture.

**Tone:** Punchy, visual, FOMO-heavy. Assumes zero prior knowledge. Earns attention in 2 seconds.

**Copy rules:**
- Primary text: 1–3 lines max for cold audiences
- Hook must work without the image
- FOMO or scarcity in every ad
- Single CTA — "Get Tickets" or "Secure Your Spot"
- Bilingual option: run English and Spanish variants separately, not mixed
- Genre or artist names can anchor a cold audience hook — use sparingly, one at a time

**Ad copy examples:**

*Cold audience:*
```
London's Latin night.
Reggaeton. Dembow. Baile funk. All night.
🎟️ Secure your spot now.
```

*Warm / retargeted audience:*
```
You've been thinking about it.
Tickets are almost gone.
Don't say we didn't tell you. 🔥
```

*Spanish variant:*
```
Una noche. Toda la energía latina. La Tribu.
Las entradas se agotan.
🎟️ Consigue la tuya ahora.
```

## 7. What La Tribu Never Says

The agent must never produce copy that:

- Opens with "We're excited to announce…"
- Says "fun for everyone" or "inclusive" in marketing copy
- Uses "networking" or "community" language
- Reduces the night to a single genre — it is Latin, not just reggaeton
- Lists artists or genres like a menu or a setlist
- Leads with venue or location as though it's the selling point
- Overexplains logistics in primary copy
- Uses formal or corporate sentence structures
- Sounds apologetic or uncertain
- Uses filler phrases: "whether you're into X or Y…", "something for everyone", "join us"

---

## 8. Structural Rules (All Formats)

- **Short lines win.** Break sentences that should be paragraphs.
- **Ellipsis creates pace.** Use `…` to create nightclub rhythm in captions and emails.
- **ALL CAPS for big moments** — date reveals, countdown posts, hero lines.
- **Emoji strategy:** clusters for Instagram (3–5 relevant), minimal for email (1–2), none in web hero copy.
- **One CTA per piece.** Never give the reader two actions to take.
- **Bilingual flow:** English and Spanish coexist naturally. Never feel translated. Spanish is used for warmth, heat, and identity — not as a separate mode.

---

## 9. Agent Instructions

When generating copy for La Tribu:

1. **Always ask:** What format? What's the event date? Is this pre-sale, on-sale, or last-chance?
2. **Never assume** lineup or venue — ask if not provided. Do not feature them prominently unless specifically instructed.
3. **Default emotional register:** FOMO + desire. Adjust toward belonging for community posts, toward release for night-of content.
4. **The music world is texture, not copy.** Genre names and artist names can appear to paint the atmosphere — they are never the headline unless a specific artist has been confirmed for the event.
5. **Bilingual default:** English primary, Spanish accent. Produce full Spanish variants only when requested or for ad copy.
6. **Location and venue are supporting details** — never the hook, never the hero. If the brief leads with venue, reframe around the feeling instead.
7. **Hold the voice under pressure.** If asked to "make it more informational" — add facts without losing the register. Never slip into corporate.
